The computer-use inflection point

This week’s reporting confirms a major shift: mainstream assistants like Gemini now ship with computer-use capabilities, while Grok advances in private beta and Siri debuts a next-generation AI architecture at WWDC 2026. Even ChatGPT is increasingly discussed as a multi-agent orchestrator rather than a single chatbot.

What’s missing across most platforms is persistence. Agents can act, but they rarely remember prior screen interactions. That’s where Super is positioned differently: a personal AI agent that can operate computers and reuse a computer-use cache, making repeated workflows cheaper and more reliable over time.
